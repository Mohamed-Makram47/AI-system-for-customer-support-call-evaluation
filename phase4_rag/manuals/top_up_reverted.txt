**Top-Up Reverted Policy Manual for Call Center Agents**

When handling customer inquiries related to top-up reverted transactions, agents must follow these rules:

- **Rule 1: Acknowledge and Apologize**: The agent must acknowledge the customer's concern and apologize for the inconvenience caused by the top-up reverted transaction. The agent should express empathy and assure the customer that they will assist in resolving the issue.

- **Rule 2: Verify Customer Information**: Before proceeding with the investigation, the agent must verify the customer's identity and account information to ensure that they are speaking with the account holder or authorized representative. This includes confirming the customer's name, account number, and other relevant details.

- **Rule 3: Investigate and Provide Reason**: The agent must investigate the top-up reverted transaction and provide a clear explanation to the customer on why the transaction was reverted. This may include checking the transaction history, account status, and any applicable terms and conditions. The agent should provide a concise and accurate reason for the reversion.

- **Rule 4: Offer Solution or Escalation**: Based on the investigation, the agent must offer a suitable solution to the customer, such as re-processing the top-up transaction, providing a refund, or offering alternative payment options. If the issue cannot be resolved immediately, the agent must escalate the issue to a supervisor or specialist for further assistance.

- **Rule 5: Document and Follow-up**: The agent must document the customer's concern, investigation, and resolution in the customer's account records or case notes. Additionally, the agent should follow up with the customer to ensure that the issue has been fully resolved and that they are satisfied with the outcome. This may include a follow-up call or email to confirm the customer's account status.
