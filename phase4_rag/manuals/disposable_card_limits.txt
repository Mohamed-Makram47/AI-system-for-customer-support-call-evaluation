**Disposable Card Limits Policy Manual for Call Center Agents**

When handling customer inquiries and concerns related to disposable card limits, agents must adhere to the following rules:

- **Verify Customer Identity**: Before providing any information or making changes to disposable card limits, agents must verify the customer's identity through our standard authentication process, which includes asking for their name, date of birth, and account information, and confirming their identity through our internal systems.

- **Explain Disposable Card Limits**: Agents must clearly explain to customers that disposable cards have specific spending limits, which are set to prevent unauthorized transactions and protect the customer's account, and that these limits may vary depending on the customer's account type and our internal policies.

- **Check Current Limits**: When a customer inquires about their disposable card limit, agents must check the current limit for that specific card by accessing the customer's account information in our system, and provide the customer with accurate and up-to-date information.

- **Escalate Limit Increase Requests**: If a customer requests to increase their disposable card limit, agents must inform them that such requests require review and approval from a supervisor or a specialized team, and provide the customer with the option to leave a callback number or have the request escalated to the relevant team for further review.

- **Document Customer Interactions**: Agents must document all interactions related to disposable card limits in our customer relationship management (CRM) system, including customer inquiries, limit checks, and escalation requests, to ensure a clear audit trail and facilitate future reference.
