**Exchange Rate Policy Manual for Call Center Agents**

The following rules are designed to ensure compliance with regulatory requirements and company policies when handling exchange rate inquiries and transactions:

- **Rule 1: Verification of Customer Identity** - Before providing any information or assistance related to exchange rates, agents must verify the identity of the customer by asking for their name, account number (if applicable), and other identifying information to ensure they are speaking with an authorized account holder or individual.

- **Rule 2: Disclosure of Exchange Rate Information** - Agents must provide clear and accurate information about current exchange rates, including any applicable fees or margins associated with the transaction. Agents should use the company's approved exchange rate resources and provide the customer with a clear understanding of the exchange rate being applied.

- **Rule 3: Compliance with Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Regulations** - Agents must comply with all applicable AML regulations when handling exchange rate transactions. This includes, but is not limited to, monitoring transactions for suspicious activity, reporting suspicious transactions to the company's AML compliance team, and ensuring that customers are not engaging in activities that could facilitate money laundering or terrorist financing.

- **Rule 4: Adherence to Exchange Rate Policies and Procedures** - Agents must adhere to the company's policies and procedures related to exchange rate transactions, including limits on transaction amounts, documentation requirements, and procedures for handling errors or discrepancies. Agents should refer to the company's policy documentation or consult with a supervisor if they are unsure about any aspect of the policy.

- **Rule 5: Documentation and Record-Keeping** - Agents must accurately document all exchange rate transactions, including the customer's request, the exchange rate applied, any fees or commissions charged, and any other relevant details. Agents must also ensure that all transactions are properly recorded and retained in accordance with the company's record-keeping policies and regulatory requirements.
