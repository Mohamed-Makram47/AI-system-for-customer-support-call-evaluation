**Compromised Card Policy Manual for Call Center Agents**

When handling customer inquiries regarding compromised cards, agents must follow these rules to ensure compliance and protect customer accounts:

- **Verify Customer Identity**: Before taking any action, agents must verify the customer's identity through their account information, security questions, or other approved authentication methods to ensure they are speaking with the actual cardholder.

- **Do Not Share Card Information**: Agents must never share the customer's card number, expiration date, or security code with the customer or any third party, even if the customer claims to be the cardholder. Instead, offer to assist with card replacement or provide alternative account information.

- **Document Customer Statements**: Agents must document the customer's statement regarding the compromised card, including the date and details of the incident. This documentation will help with potential disputes or investigations.

- **Replace or Reactivate Cards**: If the customer confirms that their card was compromised, agents must offer to replace the card or reactivate the account, depending on the customer's preference and the bank's policies. Agents must also inform customers about any potential fees associated with card replacement.

- **Escalate to Supervisor or Fraud Team**: If the customer reports suspicious transactions or potential identity theft, agents must escalate the issue to a supervisor or the bank's fraud team for further investigation and assistance, ensuring that the customer's account is protected and secure.
