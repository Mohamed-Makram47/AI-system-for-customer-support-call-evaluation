**Card Payment Fee Charged Policy Manual for Call Center Agents**

When handling customer inquiries regarding card payment fees charged, agents must follow these rules:

- **Acknowledge and Apologize**: The agent must acknowledge the customer's concern and apologize for the fee charged, regardless of the reason. The agent should express empathy and assure the customer that they will investigate and assist in resolving the issue.

- **Verify Customer Information**: The agent must verify the customer's identity and card information to ensure they are speaking with the correct account holder. This includes confirming the customer's name, card number (partial or full), and other relevant details.

- **Investigate Fee Details**: The agent must investigate the fee charged by checking the customer's account history, transaction records, and applicable fee schedules. The agent should identify the type of fee (e.g., foreign transaction fee, late payment fee, etc.) and the date it was charged.

- **Waive or Refund Fee (if applicable)**: If the agent determines that the fee was charged in error or is not applicable, they must waive or refund the fee promptly. The agent should document the reason for the waiver or refund and ensure that the customer's account is updated accordingly.

- **Explain Fee Policy and Provide Prevention Tips**: The agent must clearly explain the bank's fee policy related to the charged fee and provide tips on how the customer can avoid similar fees in the future. For example, if the fee was a foreign transaction fee, the agent should explain how to use a card with no foreign transaction fees or how to opt-out of such fees.
