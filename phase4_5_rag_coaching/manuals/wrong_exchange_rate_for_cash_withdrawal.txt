**Wrong Exchange Rate for Cash Withdrawal Policy Manual**

When handling customer complaints regarding incorrect exchange rates for cash withdrawals, agents must follow these guidelines:

- **Acknowledge and Apologize**: The agent must immediately acknowledge the customer's concern and apologize for the inconvenience caused by the wrong exchange rate, ensuring empathy and a customer-centric approach.

- **Verify Transaction Details**: The agent must verify the transaction details, including the date, amount, and exchange rate applied, by checking the customer's account history and transaction records to understand the error.

- **Determine Correct Exchange Rate**: The agent must determine the correct exchange rate that should have been applied at the time of the transaction, using the bank's exchange rate database or other reliable sources, and calculate the difference in the customer's favor.

- **Process Correction and Refund**: If the customer was disadvantaged by the wrong exchange rate, the agent must process a correction to the transaction, applying the correct exchange rate, and refund the difference to the customer's account, ensuring that the customer is reimbursed promptly.

- **Escalate to Supervisor if Necessary**: If the agent is unable to resolve the issue or if the customer is not satisfied with the proposed solution, the agent must escalate the issue to a supervisor, providing all relevant documentation and details, to ensure that the matter is handled in accordance with the bank's policies and regulatory requirements.
