- Rule 1: Check Transfer Status: Verify the current status of the pending transfer by checking the transfer details in the customer's account records.

- Rule 2: Provide Transfer Timeframe: Inform the customer of the estimated timeframe for the transfer to complete, based on the transfer type (e.g., domestic, international, EU), and specify that it may take up to 3-5 business days for international transfers and 1-2 business days for domestic transfers.

- Rule 3: Verify Customer Information: Confirm with the customer that the account information, including the recipient's account details, is correct and matches the information on file.

- Rule 4: Investigate Transfer Delay: If the transfer has been pending for an extended period (more than 5 business days for domestic transfers or 10 business days for international transfers), investigate the cause of the delay and provide a detailed explanation to the customer.

- Rule 5: Offer Additional Assistance: If the customer is still experiencing issues with the transfer, offer to research the transfer further or provide additional assistance, such as contacting the recipient's bank or providing a reference number for the transfer.

- Rule 6: Provide Tracking Information: If available, provide the customer with tracking information, such as a transfer reference number or a tracking URL, to allow them to monitor the status of the transfer.
