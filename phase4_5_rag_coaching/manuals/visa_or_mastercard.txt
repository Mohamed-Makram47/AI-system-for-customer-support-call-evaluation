**Visa or Mastercard Policy Manual for Call Center Agents**

When handling customer inquiries or issues related to Visa or Mastercard, agents must follow these guidelines:

- **Verify Cardholder Identity**: Before providing any information or assistance related to a Visa or Mastercard account, agents must verify the caller's identity. This can be done by asking for the cardholder's name, card number (partial or full, depending on the situation), expiration date, and/or the CVV (Card Verification Value) to ensure the caller is the authorized account holder or an authorized representative.

- **Adhere to Card Network Rules and Regulations**: Agents must comply with the rules and regulations set forth by the respective card networks (Visa or Mastercard) and any applicable laws, including but not limited to, the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS) and the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA). Agents should be aware of and follow specific guidelines related to card activation, transaction disputes, and account holds.

- **Provide Clear and Accurate Information**: When addressing customer inquiries or concerns about their Visa or Mastercard, agents must provide clear, concise, and accurate information. This includes explaining card benefits, fees, and any limitations or restrictions associated with the card. Agents should avoid making statements that could be misleading or confusing.

- **Document All Interactions**: Agents must document all interactions with customers regarding their Visa or Mastercard accounts in the bank's customer relationship management (CRM) system or designated platform. This includes details about the customer's inquiry, resolution provided, and any follow-up actions required. Accurate documentation helps ensure continuity of service and compliance with regulatory requirements.

- **Escalate Complex Issues**: If an agent encounters a complex issue or dispute related to a Visa or Mastercard account that they are not authorized to resolve, they must escalate the issue to a supervisor or a specialized team (e.g., fraud department, card disputes team) according to established procedures.
