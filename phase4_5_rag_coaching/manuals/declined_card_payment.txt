Here are 5 clear rules for agents handling declined card payment issues:

- **Rule 1: Verify Caller Identity**: Before providing any assistance, the agent must verify the caller's identity by asking for their name, card number (partial or full, as permitted by company policy), and/or other identifying information to ensure they are speaking with the cardholder or an authorized representative.

- **Rule 2: Run a New Transaction Check**: The agent must run a new transaction check to confirm that the card has not been reactivated or if the issue persists, to gather more information on the declined transaction, including the reason code and any available details on the decline.

- **Rule 3: Provide Clear Explanation and Reason Code**: The agent must clearly explain the reason for the card decline to the caller, providing the specific reason code (if available) and any next steps to resolve the issue, such as contacting the card issuer or providing additional information to reactivate the card.

- **Rule 4: Document Caller Interactions**: The agent must document all interactions with the caller in the customer's account records, including any attempts to resolve the issue, promises made, and actions taken, to ensure a clear audit trail and facilitate follow-up activities.

- **Rule 5: Escalate to Supervisor or Specialist (if needed)**: If the agent is unable to resolve the issue or if the caller requests to speak with a supervisor or specialist, the agent must escalate the issue promptly, providing all relevant details and documentation to ensure a seamless handover and efficient resolution.
