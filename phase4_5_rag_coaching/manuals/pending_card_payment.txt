- Rule 1: Pending Payment Explanation: Provide a clear explanation to the customer that a pending payment means the transaction is being processed and has not yet been fully cleared, and that it may take 2-3 business days to complete.

- Rule 2: Pending Duration Inquiry: If the customer inquires about the duration of the pending payment, inform them that the standard processing time for card payments is 2-3 business days, but may vary depending on the bank or card issuer.

- Rule 3: Investigation of Delayed Payment: If the customer's payment has been pending for more than 5 business days, initiate an investigation by checking the transaction status in the system and verify if there are any holds or issues on the card.

- Rule 4: Provide Estimated Resolution Time: If the investigation reveals a delay, provide the customer with an estimated time for when the payment will be processed, and offer to follow up with them if the issue is not resolved within that timeframe.

- Rule 5: Transaction Details Disclosure: Disclose to the customer the transaction details, including the date, amount, and merchant information, to help them understand the pending payment.

- Rule 6: Follow-up Action: If the agent is unable to resolve the issue during the call, inform the customer that they will follow up with them within a specified timeframe (e.g., 24-48 hours) to provide an update on the status of their payment.
