Here are 5 clear rules for agents handling declined transfers:

- **Rule 1: Acknowledge and Apologize**: When a customer calls about a declined transfer, the agent must acknowledge the issue and apologize for the inconvenience within the first 30 seconds of the call. The agent must say, "I apologize for the inconvenience you've experienced with your transfer being declined. I'd be happy to help you understand what happened."

- **Rule 2: Verify Customer Information**: Before providing any information or assistance, the agent must verify the customer's identity using their name, account number, and date of birth. The agent must confirm that the customer's information matches the information on file.

- **Rule 3: Explain Reason for Decline**: The agent must clearly explain the reason for the declined transfer, citing the specific policy or regulation that led to the decline. If the decline was due to a security block or suspicious activity, the agent must inform the customer that the block has been placed to protect their account and that they will work with them to resolve the issue.

- **Rule 4: Provide Alternatives or Next Steps**: Depending on the reason for the decline, the agent must provide the customer with alternative solutions or next steps to resolve the issue. For example, if the transfer was declined due to insufficient funds, the agent must inform the customer of their available balance and offer to assist with a deposit or transfer from another account.

- **Rule 5: Document and Escalate (if necessary)**: The agent must document the customer's concern and the resolution provided in the customer's account notes. If the issue cannot be resolved during the initial call, or if the customer is not satisfied with the resolution, the agent must escalate the issue to a supervisor or specialized team for further review and assistance. The agent must also provide the customer with a reference number or a case number for future follow-up.
