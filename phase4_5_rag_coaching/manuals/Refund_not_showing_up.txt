**Refund Not Showing Up Policy Manual for Call Center Agents**

When handling customer inquiries regarding refunds not showing up, agents must follow these 5 rules:

- **Verify Customer Information**: The agent must verify the customer's identity and account information before providing any assistance. This includes confirming the customer's name, account number, and the reason for the refund.

- **Check Refund Status**: The agent must check the status of the refund using the bank's internal systems or contacting the relevant department (e.g., credit card or loan department) to determine if the refund has been processed and when it was sent.

- **Escalate to Supervisor if Over 30 Days**: If the refund was issued over 30 days ago and still hasn't shown up, the agent must escalate the issue to their supervisor for further investigation and resolution. The supervisor will review the case and contact the customer to provide a resolution.

- **Provide Clear Explanation and Next Steps**: The agent must provide a clear explanation to the customer regarding the status of their refund, including any next steps that will be taken to resolve the issue. The agent must also provide a timeline for when the customer can expect to receive the refund or when they will be contacted with an update.

- **Document All Interactions**: The agent must document all interactions with the customer, including any checks performed, conversations had, and resolutions offered. The documentation must include the customer's account number, the reason for the refund, and the outcome of the inquiry. This documentation will be used to track the issue and ensure that all necessary steps are taken to resolve it.
