**Transfer Not Received by Recipient Policy Manual**

As a banking call center compliance expert, it is essential to ensure that agents handle the issue of transfer not received by recipient in a professional and compliant manner. The following rules must be followed:

- **Rule 1: Acknowledge and Apologize**: The agent must acknowledge the customer's concern and apologize for the inconvenience caused by the transfer not being received by the recipient. The agent must express empathy and assure the customer that they will investigate the matter further.

- **Rule 2: Gather Required Information**: The agent must gather all required information from the customer, including the transfer date, amount, recipient's name and account information, and the reference number (if any). The agent must verify the customer's identity and ensure that they have the necessary permissions to inquire about the transfer.

- **Rule 3: Investigate and Track the Transfer**: The agent must investigate the transfer by tracking its status using the bank's internal systems and contacting the relevant departments (e.g., wire transfer, ACH, or international transfer teams). The agent must document all findings and actions taken during the investigation.

- **Rule 4: Provide a Resolution or Next Steps**: The agent must provide a resolution or next steps to the customer, which may include re-initiating the transfer, canceling the transfer, or providing a refund. The agent must explain the reason for the delay or failure to deliver the transfer and provide a clear timeline for resolution.

- **Rule 5: Document and Escalate (if necessary)**: The agent must document all customer interactions, investigation findings, and resolutions in the bank's customer relationship management (CRM) system or other designated systems. If the issue cannot be resolved within the standard service level agreement (SLA) or if the customer is not satisfied with the resolution, the agent must escalate the issue to a senior agent or a specialized team (e.g., complaints or complex issues team) for further review and resolution.
