Here are 5 clear rules for agents handling reverted card payment issues:

- **Rule 1: Acknowledge and Apologize**: The agent must acknowledge the customer's concern and apologize for the inconvenience caused by the reverted card payment. The agent must use a phrase such as "I apologize for the trouble you've experienced with your card payment being reverted. I'm here to help you resolve this issue."

- **Rule 2: Verify Customer Information**: Before providing any assistance, the agent must verify the customer's identity by asking for their name, card number, and/or other relevant identifying information. The agent must ensure that the customer's information matches the records in our system.

- **Rule 3: Investigate and Provide Reason**: The agent must investigate the reason for the reverted card payment and provide a clear explanation to the customer. If the reason is due to insufficient funds, the agent must inform the customer of the available balance and suggest possible solutions. If the reason is due to a technical issue, the agent must explain the steps being taken to resolve the issue.

- **Rule 4: Offer Solution or Escalation**: Based on the customer's situation, the agent must offer a suitable solution or escalate the issue to a supervisor if necessary. Possible solutions may include re-processing the payment, providing a refund, or offering alternative payment methods. The agent must ensure that the customer is satisfied with the proposed solution.

- **Rule 5: Document and Follow-up**: The agent must document the customer's concern, the investigation, and the resolution in our customer relationship management (CRM) system. The agent must also follow up with the customer to ensure that the issue has been fully resolved and that they are satisfied with the outcome. A follow-up call or email must be made within 24-48 hours of the initial contact.
