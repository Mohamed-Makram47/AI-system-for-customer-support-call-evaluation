**Card Payment Wrong Exchange Rate Policy Manual**

When handling customer complaints regarding incorrect exchange rates applied to card transactions, agents must follow these rules:

- **Acknowledge and Apologize**: The agent must acknowledge the customer's concern, apologize for the mistake, and assure the customer that they will do their best to resolve the issue promptly.

- **Verify Transaction Details**: The agent must verify the transaction details, including the date, amount, and exchange rate applied, to understand the discrepancy and confirm the customer's claim.

- **Check Exchange Rate History**: The agent must check the historical exchange rates for the specific currency and date range to determine the correct exchange rate that should have been applied, using the bank's approved exchange rate sources.

- **Correct the Error and Provide a Clear Explanation**: If the customer's claim is valid, the agent must correct the error by processing a reversal of the incorrect transaction and re-processing the transaction at the correct exchange rate, providing a clear explanation of the correction to the customer.

- **Escalate to Supervisor if Necessary**: If the agent is unable to resolve the issue or if the customer is not satisfied with the proposed solution, the agent must escalate the issue to a supervisor, who will review the case and provide a final resolution, ensuring that the customer's concerns are addressed and that the bank's policies and procedures are followed.
