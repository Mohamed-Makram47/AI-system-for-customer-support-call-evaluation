**Lost or Stolen Card Policy Manual for Call Center Agents**

When handling customer inquiries regarding lost or stolen cards, agents must follow these 5 rules:

- **Verify Customer Identity**: Before providing any assistance, agents must verify the customer's identity by asking for their name, date of birth, and account information (e.g., account number or debit/credit card number). The agent must then use the customer's responses to authenticate their identity through our internal systems.

- **Acknowledge and Apologize**: Agents must acknowledge the customer's concern and apologize for the inconvenience caused by the lost or stolen card. A standard script may be used: "I understand that your card is lost/stolen, and I'm here to help. I apologize for the inconvenience this has caused you."

- **Immediate Card Block**: Upon confirmation of a lost or stolen card, agents must immediately block the card to prevent any unauthorized transactions. Agents must use our internal system to block the card and note the customer's account for potential future review.

- **Replacement Card Process**: Agents must explain to the customer the process for obtaining a replacement card, including any required documentation (e.g., police report) and estimated timeframe for delivery. Agents must also provide the customer with a tracking number or reference number for the replacement card request.

- **Fraud Liability Disclosure**: Agents must inform customers that they are not liable for unauthorized transactions made on their account if they report the lost or stolen card promptly. Agents must also provide information on our bank's fraud policies and procedures, including any necessary next steps for disputing unauthorized transactions.
