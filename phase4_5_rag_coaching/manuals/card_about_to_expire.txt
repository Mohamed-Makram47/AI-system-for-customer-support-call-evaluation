**Card About to Expire Policy Manual for Call Center Agents**

When handling customer inquiries regarding cards that are about to expire, agents must follow these guidelines to ensure compliance and excellent customer service:

- **Verify Customer Identity**: Before providing any information or assistance related to a card that is about to expire, agents must verify the customer's identity using their name, date of birth, and/or account information to ensure they are speaking with the authorized cardholder.

- **Provide Clear Expiration Information**: Agents must clearly explain the expiration date of the card, the reason for the expiration, and the process for replacing the card. They should also inform customers that their account information, including the credit limit and interest rate, will remain the same.

- **Offer Card Replacement Options**: Agents must offer customers the option to request a replacement card, which can typically be mailed to them automatically. If the customer requests a replacement card, the agent must confirm the customer's current address and provide an estimated timeframe for delivery.

- **Advise on Continued Card Usage**: Agents must advise customers that they can continue to use their current card until the expiration date, but not after, as merchants may not accept it. They should also remind customers to update any recurring payment information, such as automatic bill payments, with their new card information once received.

- **Document Customer Interactions**: Agents must document all customer interactions related to card expirations in the customer's account records, including any requests for replacement cards, address updates, or other relevant details. This ensures a clear audit trail and helps prevent potential disputes or issues.
