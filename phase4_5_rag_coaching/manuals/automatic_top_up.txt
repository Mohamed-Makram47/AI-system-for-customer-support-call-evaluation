**Automatic Top-Up Policy Manual for Call Center Agents**

When handling customer inquiries and requests related to Automatic Top-Up, agents must follow these rules:

- **Verify Customer Eligibility**: Before enrolling a customer in Automatic Top-Up, agents must verify that the customer has a qualifying account type and meets the minimum balance requirement as specified in the bank's policies. Agents must check the customer's account details and inform them of their eligibility status.

- **Clearly Explain Terms and Conditions**: Agents must clearly explain the terms and conditions of Automatic Top-Up to the customer, including the amount that will be transferred, the frequency of transfers, and any associated fees. Agents must ensure that customers understand that they are authorizing recurring transfers from their linked account.

- **Obtain Customer Consent**: Agents must obtain explicit consent from the customer before enrolling them in Automatic Top-Up. This includes confirming the customer's understanding of the terms and conditions and ensuring that they are authorizing the transfers. Agents must document the customer's consent in the customer's account records.

- **Handle Customer Opt-Out Requests**: When a customer requests to opt out of Automatic Top-Up, agents must promptly process the request and confirm with the customer that the recurring transfers have been stopped. Agents must also verify that any pending transfers are cancelled and that the customer's account is updated accordingly.

- **Escalate Complex Issues**: If a customer disputes a transfer or raises a complex issue related to Automatic Top-Up, agents must escalate the issue to a senior agent or a compliance specialist for review and resolution. Agents must not attempt to resolve complex issues on their own and must ensure that all disputes are thoroughly investigated and documented.
