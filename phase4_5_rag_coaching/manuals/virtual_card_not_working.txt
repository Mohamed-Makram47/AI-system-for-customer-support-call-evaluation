Here are 5 clear rules for agents handling the issue of a virtual card not working:

- **Verify Customer Information**: Before troubleshooting the virtual card issue, the agent must verify the customer's identity by asking for their name, date of birth, and the last 4 digits of their account number or social security number.

- **Check Virtual Card Status**: The agent must check the status of the virtual card in the customer's account to determine if it is active, expired, or has been reported lost or stolen.

- **Troubleshoot Technical Issues**: If the virtual card is active, the agent must troubleshoot technical issues by asking the customer to confirm their device and browser information, and checking if the customer is using a supported browser or device.

- **Escalate to Specialist if Necessary**: If the issue cannot be resolved through troubleshooting, the agent must escalate the issue to a specialist, such as a technical support agent or a fraud investigator, if the customer reports suspicious activity or if the issue appears to be related to a technical glitch.

- **Document Resolution Attempts**: The agent must document all attempts to resolve the virtual card issue, including the customer's reported issue, the steps taken to troubleshoot, and the resolution or escalation outcome, in the customer's account notes or case management system.
